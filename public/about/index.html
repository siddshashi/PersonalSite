About Me

I’m an undergrad studying EECS and French at UC Berkeley. I’m interested in developing tools to bridge communication gaps and transfer ideas, particularly through multimodal models and LLM inference.

Last semester, I worked at a stealth robotics startup, where I built and deployed an ultra-low latency ranging system. Before that, I spent time writing software at Rivian and conducting research at BAIR. Also, for two years, I built software and hardware for autonomous formula-style electric race cars with Formula Electric at Berkeley.

I enjoy emails, feel free to contribute to this enjoyment via sshashi at berkeley dot edu.
